02

The method

Bodywork built on sport science

Licensed massage therapists with kinesiology and exercise-science degrees, working with tour-level competitors, weekend runners, and everyone between. Not a spa. Not a franchise. A studio with a method.

  • A.

    Assess

    We watch how you move before we touch the table. Sport, training block, injury history — the session is built from that, not from a script.

  • B.

    Treat

    Targeted sports massage and athletic rehabilitation — deep, specific work on the tissue and the pattern that keeps loading it.

  • C.

    Coach

    You leave with homework: form corrections, mobility work, and a plan for the next training block. The goal is fewer visits, not more.

The therapists

Who's on the table side

  • Mike Martin

    LMT · BSKin · MMP

    Mike has been in the professional fitness world since a college internship turned into a career. Originally headed for physical therapy, he switched to kinesiology with a focus on sport and exercise science. Six years, three gyms, and a lot of learning as a personal trainer led him to sports massage and athletic rehabilitation.

  • Brian Gscheidle

    LMT · MsK · CPT · MMP

    Brian has dedicated his life to serving the community and promoting healthy patterns of movement. His passion began with his own weight-loss journey in high school — over 50 pounds in a single summer. After undergrad and grad school in kinesiology at Augusta University, he pursued massage therapy as a more direct way to help people return to their sports and lives after injury.

  • Brennan Kirby

    LMT

    Brennan graduated from NC Massage School in North Carolina. Her modalities are Swedish, deep tissue, and sports recovery and maintenance. When she's not in the studio, she's out on distance runs or spending time with her husband and two kids.
